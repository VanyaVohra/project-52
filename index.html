Interesting facts about Chameleons

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  • They have extremely powerful tongues.
  • They just live for 2-10 years.
  • They change color in order to communicate or to regulate their body temperature

Interesting facts about Camels

  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.
  • Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.
  • There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.

Interesting facts about Cicadas

  • Cicadas can be black, brown or green and can have red, white or blue eyes.
  • Cicadas can live a relatively long time: 4 to 17 years
  • A group of singing cicadas is called a chorus.
  • Some cicada calls can be heard up to 1 mile away

Interesting facts about Pandas

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.
  • A panda Can Poop 28 Kilos per day.

Interesting facts about Pigeons

  • Large pigeons can reach 19 inches in length and 8.8 pounds of weight.
  • Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second.
  • Pigeons were used as mail carriers during the First and Second World War.
  • Pigeons can recognize all letters of the English alphabet.

Interesting facts about Teddies

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • Washable teddy bears didn't appear until 1954 when the stuffing was switched to an easier to clean nylon fill.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
